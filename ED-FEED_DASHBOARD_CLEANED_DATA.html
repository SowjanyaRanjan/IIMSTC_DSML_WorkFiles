AI-Generated Insights
Powered by NLP pipeline · BERT sentiment analysis
🔒 All insights are generated on anonymized data. No student identifiers are used in AI processing.
💪
Main Strengths
Students consistently appreciate faculty who provide clear explanations and are accessible outside class hours.
Practical, application-focused teaching styles receive significantly higher positive sentiment scores.
Faculty rated "Inspirational" correlate with 78%+ positive feedback rate across all departments.
Courses with "Gives Good Feedback" tag show the highest student engagement and retention signals.
⚠️
Main Problems
Computer Science and Economics departments show negative sentiment exceeding 39%, above the alert threshold.
Repeated complaints about "tough grading" and lack of clarity in assignment expectations.
Students report difficulty understanding lecture material when pacing is too fast, especially in STEM courses.
Several departments show limited attendance flexibility, leading to frustration among working students.
🚀
Suggested Improvements
Establish peer-review programs for faculty in departments with >35% negative sentiment.
Introduce standardized grading rubrics — especially for writing-heavy courses — to reduce grade-related complaints.
Encourage office hours participation by tying availability scores to faculty review cycles.
Roll out hybrid teaching tools for departments with online student cohorts showing lower satisfaction.
